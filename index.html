Project Goal and Plan

In this project, we will analyze the personal stock portfolios of its members: Arzaan Singh and Ethan Kraus. We will use 10 year historical price data from all of our stocks, along with financial research data (in particular, Fama-French's 5 Factor Model) to understand the different explanations for returns in our portfolios with respect to the US market.

Link to research data: https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/data_library.html.

Stock Price Data from CapitalIQ

Our goal will be to (1) extrapolate coefficients of Fama-French's 5 Factor Model (FF5) for each of our portfolios, (2) understand the the coefficients, (3) create a prediction model in order to estimate how these stocks will perform over the foreseeable future, and (4) compare our portfolios estimated returns in order to determine which group member has the better stock portfolio based on this data.

Currently, we have extracted our baseline data which we will use to check for the validity of our model and understand the data before applying it to our personal portfolios. This includes the FF5 Model data which we will use in our regressions and descriptive/predictive models. We have also loaded S&P 500 Total Return data from CapIQ and used a few summary statistics to understand underlying trends. As next steps, we will use this data to conduct our first multi-linear regression and find the correlation matrix (below). After which, we will load our company data for our portfolios and weight them accordingly for consistency and filling in any NaN values. Using these data sets we will conduct our analysis. Graphs and summary statistics are below.

Research Question

Using historical stock price data along with FF5, can we determine which of our 2 group members is more likely to have a higher performing personal stock portfolio over the forseeable future?
